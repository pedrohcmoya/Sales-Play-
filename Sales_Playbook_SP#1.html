Sales Play Introduction · 1.1
Sales Play Definition
What a Sales Play is, why it works, and how it differs from business-as-usual selling
What It Is
A Focused Commercial Sprint
A Sales Play is a structured, time-boxed initiative with a specific product, a specific account list, a specific team, and a defined sequence of actions — all engineered to maximize conversion in the shortest possible window.
Why It Works
Structure Drives Results
A Sales Play gives every AM the same ammunition — the right message, the right script, the right collateral — so the quality of the engagement is consistently high across the team.
How It Differs from BAU
Targeted vs. Broad Coverage
Business as usual means managing a full portfolio reactively. A Sales Play means proactively pursuing a defined list with a defined motion — prioritized, scripted, and tracked with dedicated cadence and leadership attention.
Expected Outcome
Faster Pipeline, Higher Win Rate
Sales Plays bring better results. The combination of focused account selection, scripted engagement, and cross-functional support (Product + Marketing) compresses the sales cycle significantly.

Discovery & Trigger Questions
Questions to guide off-script conversations — uncover pain points, assess urgency, and move toward a decision
Discovery Questions
• Who is your current IP transit provider and how long have you been with them?
• Are you running a single provider or do you have multiple transit suppliers today?
Trigger Questions
• If your current provider announced they were exiting your market or sunsetting a key route tomorrow, how long would it take you to activate an alternative?
• Have you ever been in a situation where your provider's internal problems became your network's problem?
Discovery Questions
• How are you currently measuring latency to your top content sources — Google, Meta, Akamai, Cloudflare?
• Have your subscribers raised complaints about video streaming quality or gaming latency in the last 12 months?
Trigger Questions
• If your latency to Google or Meta caches is 20–30ms higher than a competitor's network, what is the subscriber churn impact you would estimate over 12 months?
• Have you ever lost a corporate or ISP client specifically because of network performance rather than price?
Discovery Questions
• How many independent routes does your network have today for IP transit?
• If your primary transit provider had a major outage right now, how long would it take for your subscribers to feel the impact?
Trigger Questions
• If your single transit provider went down for 4 hours during peak traffic, what would the direct revenue and reputational impact be for your business?
• Have you calculated what a 2-hour full outage costs you in subscriber compensation, churn, and brand damage?
Discovery Questions
• Is your current pricing based on a fixed commit or a burstable model?
• When did you last run a competitive benchmark on your cost-per-Mbps against the current market?
Trigger Questions
• If you could reduce your cost-per-Mbps by 15–20% on your highest-volume routes without any performance tradeoff, how would that change your investment capacity for network expansion?
• Are your current contract terms flexible enough to scale with your growth, or are you paying for committed capacity you are not always using?

Objection Management
Framework for handling the most frequent objections — with suggested responses and exploration questions
🔴We had a bad experience with Cirion's network and support in the past▼
How to Respond
  • Cirion has restructured its NOC and escalation protocols — the team and processes behind 400G today are not the same as what you experienced before
  • If the issue was on the network side: dedicated project managers now oversee each activation with defined SLAs and milestone checkpoints
  • If the issue was on the support side: a named account manager is your single point of contact — you call one person, not a queue
Explore
Was the issue on the network side, the support side during incidents, or both? Is that experience still influencing the current team's view or has there been turnover since then?
🔴We have had billing issues with Cirion — some invoices didn't match what was agreed▼
How to Respond
  • The 400G rate card is fully transparent — the billing model is chosen upfront and locked into the contract with no hidden variables
  • A named account manager is accountable for every invoice
  • We can add billing review checkpoints into the contract itself, so any adjustment is flagged and agreed before the next cycle closes
Explore
Is there a specific line item or charge that was unclear? Would a monthly billing summary report give your team the visibility it needs?
🔴There are several other players with similar offers▼
How to Respond
  • Cirion owns its backbone end-to-end — fewer hops between LatAm points means lower latency
  • No hidden third-party costs embedded in the price
  • Cirion's infrastructure means you are buying capacity on a modern purpose-built network, not legacy equipment being sweated for margin
Explore
Have you done a full route-by-route latency comparison recently? Is the pricing pressure coming from your own cost targets?
🔴The structure does not attend all my needs▼
How to Respond
  • Cirion's Metro 3 expansion is actively adding 400G-capable PoPs across secondary cities in Argentina, Peru, Colombia, and Brazil
  • For locations where our owned footprint does not yet reach, fiber exchange agreements with local carriers extend coverage at near-net economics — not expensive rates
Explore
Is this a current requirement or part of an expansion plan? If we confirm the geography coverage, is that the only blocker or are there other requirements?
🔴We are already covered by another provider — why would we change?▼
How to Respond
  • Single-supplier dependency on critical infrastructure like IP transit is a risk — Cirion on a second route or second geography gives you redundancy
  • Your current provider may still be running legacy infrastructure on key routes
  • The right starting point is a route-by-route latency comparison to your top content sources, so the conversation is grounded in data rather than preference
Explore
How many transit providers are you currently running? When did you last run a competitive benchmark on your current provider's latency performance?
